Albums

Seven studio albums so far...

Showbiz is the debut studio album by Muse, released in the United Kingdom on 4 October 1999.
The album was produced by John Leckie and Paul Reeve in conjunction with the band.
Showbiz was a moderate commercial success.

Origin of Symmetry is the second studio album by Muse released on 17 July 2001.
The album was a critical and commercial success.
The title and theme for the album comes from a concept put forward by the theoretical physicist Michio Kaku in his book Hyperspace.

Absolution is the third studio album by Muse. It was released on 15 September 2003.
The album followed up on Origin of Symmetry's diverse musical tendencies and elaborate sound, while also having a more focused and consistent theme and aesthetic throughout.
Absolution has a noticeably darker and heavier tone musically, with a lyrical focus on the theological and apocalyptic concepts.

Black Holes and Revelations is the fourth studio album by Muse, released on 3 July 2006.
Recording was split between New York and France, and it was the first time Muse had taken a more active role in the album's production.
The album was a change in style from Muse's previous albums, and the band cited influences that included Depeche Mode, Millionaire, Lightning Bolt, Sly and the Family Stone, and music from southern Italy.

The Resistance is the fifth studio album by Muse, released in Europe on 14 September 2009.
Upon its release, it topped the album charts in 19 countries. It also debuted at number three on the Billboard 200 chart, selling 128,000 copies during the first week.
Critics were mostly complimentary about the album, with much of the praise directed towards its ambition and classical music influences.

The 2nd Law is the sixth studio album by Muse. It was released on 28 September 2012.
The title of the album refers to the second law of thermodynamics and the album's cover art features a map of the human brain's pathways, which was taken from the Human Connectome Project.

Drones is the seventh studio album by Muse, released on June 2015.
It was recorded between October 2014 and April 2015 at The Warehouse Studio in Vancouver, British Columbia, and co-produced by the band and Robert John "Mutt" Lange.
Muse aimed to return to the simpler guitar-bass-drums rock sound of their earlier albums.

Others

Muse is the debut extended play (EP) by Muse.
Limited to 999 hand-numbered copies, it was released on 11 May 1998.
Three of the EP's four songs – "Overdue", "Cave" and "Escape" – were re-recorded for the band's full-length debut album Showbiz.

Muscle Museum is the second extended play (EP) by Muse.
Four of the EP's five original songs – "Muscle Museum", "Sober", "Uno" and "Unintended" – were re-recorded for the band's full-length debut, Showbiz.

Random 1-8 is the third extended play (EP) by Muse.
The EP was released in Japan to promote the band's tour.
It consists of six B-sides recorded during the Showbiz era, and two live tracks. There are also three remixes of "Sunburn" after the last track.

Hullabaloo Soundtrack, also known as Hullabaloo, is a compilation album by Muse and the soundtrack to their live video album Hullabaloo: Live at Le Zenith, Paris.
The album comprises one disc of previously released B-sides and a second disc of live recordings from the band's performances at Le Zénith in Paris, France on 28 and 29 October 2001.

HAARP is the third live album and video by Muse, released in 2008.
The CD documents the band's performance at London's Wembley Stadium on 16 June 2007, while the DVD contains 20 tracks from the performance on 17 June.

Live at Rome Olympic Stadium is a live album by Muse, released on November 2013.
The concert was a part of The Unsustainable Tour, which is a moniker for the band's summer 2013 European leg of The 2nd Law World Tour.

New Kind of Kick is a cover of a song by The Cramps that was done as a Halloween special in 2016.

Dig Down is the first glimpse of the eighth studio album.
This new single was released on 18 May 2017.
